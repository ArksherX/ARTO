SECTION 3: PRESENTATION ABSTRACT (Max 1337 characters)
================================================================================

Autonomous AI agents are no longer experimental—they're in production, with 
access to credentials, tools, and decision-making authority. While we've 
focused on prompt injection, a more dangerous threat has emerged: goal 
hijacking, where agents are manipulated into abandoning their original 
objectives through semantic drift.

This talk introduces VerityFlux 2.0, the first OWASP-aligned AI security 
framework with 100% coverage of OWASP LLM Top 10 (2025) and Agentic AI Top 10 
(2026). We demonstrate how baseline agents have a 50% goal hijacking failure 
rate, and how VerityFlux reduces this to <10% through semantic drift detection.

Beyond detection, we present the Cognitive Firewall—a runtime protection system 
that validates agent intent, enforces permission boundaries, and predicts 
cascading failures. Live demos show real-time blocking of goal hijacking, tool 
misuse, and memory poisoning attacks. The Flight Recorder component provides 
complete state snapshots for GDPR, SOC 2, and ISO 27001 compliance.

We share production case studies (40% attack surface reduction in healthcare) 
and provide the complete open-source framework, including CI/CD integration, 
web interface, and deployment guides. The talk concludes with 2026 threat 
predictions: kill switches, multi-modal attacks, and the evolution toward 
enterprise-grade AI security.
